Apps & Platforms

Shared Tools

A curated collection of tools I use and recommend across my courses — from AI chatbots to audience response systems and field identification apps.

🤖

AI Chatbots & Assistants

🟠

Claude (Anthropic)

My top recommendation for academic work. Known for accuracy, thoughtful reasoning, and features like artifacts, projects, and writing styles. Excellent for developing assessments, creating materials, and providing detailed feedback.

AI Chatbot
claude.ai
💬

ChatGPT (OpenAI)

Text and voice prompting, image and video creation. Widely used by students — understanding its capabilities helps you design better assignments and AI policies. Free tier available; Plus subscription for advanced features.

AI Chatbot
chatgpt.com
✨

Google Gemini

Text-prompting with web-integrated responses and image creation. Integrates with Google Workspace. Useful for students using Google Drive and Docs. Currently free with generous access for educators and students.

AI Chatbot
gemini.google.com
📓

Google NotebookLM

Works exclusively with content you provide — upload up to 50 files (PDFs, audio, URLs, Google Drive). Generates study guides, FAQs, practice quizzes, and audio overviews from your course materials. Free and genuinely powerful for students.

AI Study Tool
notebooklm.google
🔍

Perplexity.ai

An AI-powered search engine that answers queries using real-time web information and includes source citations. No account required. Great for quickly finding class activities, recent research, or teaching strategies with academic backing.

AI Search
perplexity.ai
🧪

My Custom AI Apps

🎓

ESS Tutor

An AI tutoring assistant tailored to ESS course content. It reviews key topics, answers student questions, and helps reinforce concepts covered in class. Free to use — no account required.

AI TutorPlaylab.ai
Open ESS Tutor
📊

Excel Guide

Helps students analyze datasets, understand formulas, and build plots for their ESS coursework. Walks through steps interactively — great for data labs and assignments involving spreadsheet work.

Data AnalysisPlaylab.ai
Open Excel Guide
📋

Rubric Maker

Generates well-structured rubrics for any type of assignment. Describe the task and the criteria you care about — the tool builds a ready-to-use rubric. Useful for instructors and students alike.

AssessmentPlaylab.ai
Open Rubric Maker

Engagement & Polling

📊

Wooclap

Live polls, word clouds, multiple-choice questions, and interactive Q&A — all from any device. My go-to for real-time engagement in large classes. Students respond on their phones; no app download required.

EngagementPolling
wooclap.com

Playlab.ai

Build and share AI-powered learning activities and simulations. I use Playlab to create project-based learning experiences that are interactive and adaptive. Great for collaborative problem-solving in class.

Field & Nature

🦋

iNaturalist

Photograph any organism and get an AI-powered species identification. I use iNaturalist in ESS 050 field trips for students to document biodiversity observations in real time. Free, crowd-verified, and genuinely fun in the field.

Course Management

🐾

Catcourses (Canvas)

UC Merced's learning management system — built on Canvas. Useful tip: add /undelete after your course number in the URL to recover deleted content. The What-If Grade Calculator is a great student-facing tool to share.
